# AnnealMusic v7.3 Plan — Sonification Library + Recipes

We are building a curated catalog of 20 canonical sonification mappings and an interactive recipe book demonstrating how to apply them across ICAD-research domains. This plan outlines the technical design, authoring strategy, module definitions, and API specifications.

---

## 1. Final Template List (20 Mappings Across 4 Families)

### Time Series (8 Mappings)

1. **Single Scalar Drift**
   - **Description**: Single scalar value over time maps to a continuous synthesis parameter (e.g. brightness or space).
   - **Academic Citation**: Hermann, T., Hunt, A., & Neuhoff, J. G. (2011). _The Sonification Handbook_.
   - **Use Case**: Climate temperature drift, stock price volatility.
2. **Multivariate Partials Ensemble**
   - **Description**: High-dimensional time series mapped to an ensemble of parallel oscillator partials.
   - **Academic Citation**: Barrass, S. (2012). _Acoustic Sonification of Multivariate Data_.
   - **Use Case**: Multi-sensor environmental arrays, EEG channel telemetry.

3. **Anomaly Punctuation Event**
   - **Description**: Triggers musical punctuation events (bells) when an anomaly score/indicator exceeds a calibrated threshold.
   - **Academic Citation**: Vickers, P. (2016). _Sonification for Network Monitoring_.
   - **Use Case**: Server intrusion detection, heartbeat arrhythmia.

4. **Spectral Mapping**
   - **Description**: Frequency-domain time series mapped across synthesis frequency bands/harmonics.
   - **Academic Citation**: Grond, F., & Hermann, T. (2011). _Aesthetic Factors in Sonification_.
   - **Use Case**: FFT ocean wave data, acoustic spectrogram playback.

5. **Triggered Partial Onsets**
   - **Description**: Discontinuous events trigger short, localized sound bursts with transient decay envelopes.
   - **Academic Citation**: Worrall, D. (2009). _An Introduction to Data Sonification_.
   - **Use Case**: High-frequency trading executions, seismic spike records.

6. **Periodicity locked Motif**
   - **Description**: Periodic time series modulate the tempo, rhythm, or a repeating melodic motif.
   - **Academic Citation**: de Campo, A. (2007). _Sonification Design Patterns_.
   - **Use Case**: Circadian rhythm tracking, planetary orbital resonance.

7. **Volatility Roughness**
   - **Description**: Volatility/rate-of-change maps to detune and FM modulation index to create sensory auditory roughness.
   - **Academic Citation**: Fritz, C., & Blackwell, A. F. (2005). _Design of a Sonification Tool_.
   - **Use Case**: Stock market volatility index (VIX), wind speed turbulence.

8. **Cumulative Drone Evolution**
   - **Description**: Slowly evolving metric accumulation maps to low-frequency phase-coupled drones.
   - **Academic Citation**: Polli, A. (2005). _Atmospherics/Weather Works_.
   - **Use Case**: Carbon emission accumulation, reservoir water levels.

---

### Scalar Fields / Spatial Data (5 Mappings)

9. **1D Spatial Pan**
   - **Description**: Spatial position maps directly to pan/frequency (position $\rightarrow$ frequency/stereo field).
   - **Academic Citation**: Neuhoff, J. G. (2004). _Ecological Acoustics_.
   - **Use Case**: Linear particle tracking, pipeline pressure distribution.

10. **2D Field Frequency-Amplitude Grid**
    - **Description**: 2D grid coordinates map to the 2-axis frequency and amplitude space of a scanning voice.
    - **Academic Citation**: Hermann, T., & Ritter, H. (1999). _Image Sonification_.
    - **Use Case**: Satellite heatmaps, agricultural moisture maps.

11. **3D Spatial Ensemble**
    - **Description**: 3D spatial field points map to a spatialized acoustic ensemble surrounding the listener (azimuth, elevation, distance).
    - **Academic Citation**: Lokki, T., & Gröhn, M. (2005). _Spatial Audio in Sonification_.
    - **Use Case**: Meteorological flight paths, localized drone sensor clusters.

12. **Gradient Field Coupling**
    - **Description**: Local spatial gradient vectors couple to frequency offsets or phase-drift coefficients.
    - **Academic Citation**: Kuramoto, Y. (1984). _Chemical Oscillations, Waves, and Turbulence_.
    - **Use Case**: Ocean temperature gradient flow, gravitational field vectors.

13. **Vector Pan-Intensity Flow**
    - **Description**: Vector fields map direction to stereo panning and magnitude to oscillator pitch/intensity.
    - **Academic Citation**: Flow-Field Sonification Practices.
    - **Use Case**: Wind vector maps, magnetic field flows.

---

### Network / Graph Data (4 Mappings)

14. **Node Degree Density**
    - **Description**: Node degree in a graph determines the harmonic complexity and density of active partials.
    - **Academic Citation**: Bovermann, T. et al. (2007). _Graph Sonification Methods_.
    - **Use Case**: Social network centrality, internet routing hub load.

15. **Edge Weight Coupling**
    - **Description**: Graph edge weights map directly to the coupling constants ($K_{ij}$) of Kuramoto phase-coupled oscillators.
    - **Academic Citation**: Kuramoto synchronization in complex networks.
    - **Use Case**: Neural connectivity maps, electrical grid sync load.

16. **Connected Component Selection**
    - **Description**: Sub-graph cluster characteristics (size, density) select different synthesis engines (e.g. waveguide, sine, bowed).
    - **Academic Citation**: Topological Data Sonification.
    - **Use Case**: Software module dependencies, cluster isolation tracking.

17. **Centrality Base Roots**
    - **Description**: Degree or eigenvector centrality assigns root frequencies from a custom microtonal tuning scale.
    - **Academic Citation**: Centrality Sonification Design.
    - **Use Case**: Supply-chain logistics bottlenecks, epidemiological super-spreaders.

---

### Structured Event Data (3 Mappings)

18. **Log Stream Granular Bursts**
    - **Description**: Text log levels (Info, Warning, Error) dynamically control granular burst duration, pitch, and density.
    - **Academic Citation**: Barra, M. et al. (2002). _Web Server Monitoring via Sonification_.
    - **Use Case**: Live Kubernetes events, API request error spikes.

19. **Categorical Engine Swap**
    - **Description**: State changes and categorical keys trigger immediate hot-swaps between sound engines.
    - **Academic Citation**: Event-based Sonification Design.
    - **Use Case**: Manufacturing assembly line state machine, smart-grid routing phases.

20. **Performance Arc Envelope**
    - **Description**: Performance curves (CPU/Memory load) modulate synthesis attack-decay envelopes and filter cutoff frequency.
    - **Academic Citation**: Auditory Display of System Health.
    - **Use Case**: Database connection pool pressure, serverless function cold starts.

## 2. Mapping Template Database Schema

We define the SQL table `mapping_templates` matching the specifications exactly:

```sql
CREATE TABLE mapping_templates (
  id              UUID PRIMARY KEY,
  slug            TEXT UNIQUE NOT NULL,
  title           TEXT NOT NULL,
  description     TEXT NOT NULL,
  domain_family   TEXT NOT NULL,                   -- 'time-series' | 'scalar-field' | 'network' | 'structured-event'
  source_schema   JSONB NOT NULL,                  -- expected columns + types
  mapping_spec    JSONB NOT NULL,                  -- default v7.1 mapping spec
  calibration_recommendation TEXT,
  citation        TEXT,
  recipe_content  TEXT NOT NULL,                   -- markdown
  example_data_path TEXT,
  example_audio_path TEXT,
  position        INTEGER NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,
  archived_at     TIMESTAMPTZ,
  created_at      TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT now()
);

CREATE INDEX idx_mapping_templates_domain ON mapping_templates(domain_family) WHERE archived_at IS NULL;
```

### SQLAlchemy Definition (`api/app/models.py`)

```python
class MappingTemplate(Base):
    __tablename__ = "mapping_templates"
    __table_args__ = (
        CheckConstraint(
            "domain_family IN ('time-series', 'scalar-field', 'network', 'structured-event')",
            name="ck_mapping_templates_domain_family",
        ),
    )

    id: Mapped[uuid.UUID] = mapped_column(GUID(), primary_key=True, default=_uuid)
    slug: Mapped[str] = mapped_column(String, unique=True, nullable=False, index=True)
    title: Mapped[str] = mapped_column(String, nullable=False)
    description: Mapped[str] = mapped_column(String, nullable=False)
    domain_family: Mapped[str] = mapped_column(String, nullable=False)
    source_schema: Mapped[dict] = mapped_column(JSONType(), nullable=False)
    mapping_spec: Mapped[dict] = mapped_column(JSONType(), nullable=False)
    calibration_recommendation: Mapped[str | None] = mapped_column(String, nullable=True)
    citation: Mapped[str | None] = mapped_column(String, nullable=True)
    recipe_content: Mapped[str] = mapped_column(String, nullable=False)
    example_data_path: Mapped[str | None] = mapped_column(String, nullable=True)
    example_audio_path: Mapped[str | None] = mapped_column(String, nullable=True)
    position: Mapped[int] = mapped_column(Integer, nullable=False, default=0)
    archived_at: Mapped[datetime | None] = mapped_column(DateTime(timezone=True), nullable=True)
    created_at: Mapped[datetime] = mapped_column(
        DateTime(timezone=True), nullable=False, server_default=func.now()
    )
```

## 3. Recipe Content Structure

Each recipe is written in markdown and includes the following required sections to ensure rigorous documentation:

- **Title & Overview**: Standard title stating the mapping and use case.
- **Use Case**: Deep-dive into a specific real-world data scenario (e.g., climate temperature drift over a century).
- **Step-by-Step Instructions**: Walkthrough of how to bind the streams, configure transforms, and calibrate.
- **Academic Citation**: Rigorous mapping history pointing to its ICAD/acoustics academic origins.
- **Honest Limitations (CRITICAL)**: An explicit section detailing what the sonification reveals AND what it obscures (e.g., extreme values masked by continuous smoothing, stereo panning bias, loss of precision compared to visual charts).

---

## 4. Citation Discipline

Citing academic origins is a strict prerequisite. Every recipe/template lists:

- Primary author, year, paper title.
- Publication venue (e.g., _International Conference on Auditory Display (ICAD)_).
- Theoretical core explanation (how the audio binding implements the paper's perceptual principles).

To ensure high-fidelity recipe drafting and seed database inserts:

- We will write a seeding script `/scripts/seed_mapping_templates.py` containing the fully articulated 20 templates and markdown recipes.
- We will implement an automated offline verification script to test that all mapping templates parse correctly, comply with schemas, and compile to coherent sonification states.
- Example data files will be seeded in `examples/sonification/data/` for easy sandbox testing.

## 8. Risks & Mitigations

1. **Editorial prose workload**: Authoring 20 dense academic recipes could bloat context.
   - _Mitigation_: We will write a programmatic Python script to seed the DB directly with highly structured content, ensuring extreme readability and completeness.
2. **Dynamic Calibration errors**: Pointing templates at custom datasets with highly varying ranges might break synthesis limits (e.g. frequencies exceeding human hearing).
   - _Mitigation_: The `from-template` endpoint and client-side instantiator will execute an auto-calibration step, inspecting the min/max bounds of the uploaded data and scaling to comfortable synthesizer ranges (e.g., 100Hz - 1000Hz).
