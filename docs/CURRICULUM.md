# The AnnealMusic Curriculum (v6.4)

This is the detailed outline of the AnnealMusic "Learn" curriculum and the
pedagogical thinking behind it. The curriculum is authored as **specs** — what
each lesson covers — and the per-step prose is generated by the v6.1 LLM pipeline
and reviewed before publish (see [LESSON_GENERATION.md](LESSON_GENERATION.md) and
[CURRICULUM_AUTHORING.md](CURRICULUM_AUTHORING.md)).

- **Source of truth:** `api/app/services/curriculum_content.py` — every track,
  lesson spec, and prerequisite edge. The seed migration (`0023`) and the
  end-to-end coherence test both consume it.
- **Scope:** 5 tracks · 55 lessons.
- **Route:** `/learn` (no URL bump in v6.4 — the curriculum is server-side).

---

## Pedagogical philosophy

1. **Hear it, then reason about it.** Every lesson has something to _hear_ — a
   live `demo` patch or an `audio-clip` — and ends with an open `reflection`.
   Concepts are grounded in sound before they are named. (Enforced by QA rule 1.)
2. **Calm by design.** No certificates, no quizzes, no scores, no streaks. The
   browser shows lesson counts and gentle prerequisite _hints_, never percentages
   or locks. Progress is private and resumable; abandonment is invisible. The
   curriculum is "as ignorable as it is interesting."
3. **Honest framing.** The science track names claims and then states the
   evidence plainly. Framing-sensitive topics (432 Hz, solfeggio, binaural beats,
   entrainment) carry the [FRAMING.md](FRAMING.md) directive at generation and are
   checked automatically — AnnealMusic supports these textures because they sound
   distinct, **not** because of any verified clinical effect.
4. **A connected graph, not a syllabus.** Lessons declare prerequisites; the
   graph is a DAG rooted at a single intro lesson. Cross-track edges let the
   physics of the harmonic series sit under the spectral engines, and the
   Kuramoto model sit under the product's own drift loop.
5. **Difficulty follows depth.** A lesson is never easier than its hardest
   prerequisite (QA rule 9), and every track has an ungated intro lesson, so a
   newcomer always has a calm place to start.

## The prerequisite graph

- **Root:** `synthesis-fundamentals/intro` — the universal entry point and the
  onboarding anchor (no prerequisites).
- **Invariant:** acyclic (a DAG), enforced by the QA DAG check and the
  graph-editor's in-editor cycle guard. Depth ≤ 5 hops.
- **Cross-track edges** (the interesting ones):
  - `music-science-crossover/harmonic-series` → `synthesis-fundamentals/fm-engine`
    and `…/additive-engine` (spectral intuition before the spectral engines).
  - `synthesis-fundamentals/physical-string` → `music-science-crossover/vibrating-strings`
    (the engine motivates the physics).
  - `synthesis-fundamentals/sculpt-model` → `music-science-crossover/phase-kuramoto`
    (the product's drift loop _is_ coupled oscillators).
  - `composition-technique/drone-mode` → `ambient-history-listening/drone-slow`.
  - `production-daw/midi-out` → `…/osc-basics`; `…/stem-export` → `…/daw-integration`.

The full edge list is `PREREQ_EDGES` in `curriculum_content.py`; it is asserted
acyclic, single-rooted, fully-reachable, and difficulty-monotonic by
`api/tests/test_curriculum_content.py`.

## Authoring & generation workflow

1. **Author the spec** (the editorial work) — or scaffold a draft with the
   **spec generator** (`#admin` → Spec generator) and refine it.
2. **Batch-generate** (`#admin` → Batch) — the v6.1 pipeline fills in each step's
   prose; unchanged specs are free cache hits.
3. **Review** (`#admin` → Review) — generation status + a QA badge per lesson,
   beside the per-step spec↔output editor. Approve, revise, regenerate, or
   manually override a step.
4. **Wire prerequisites** (`#admin` → Prerequisites) — drag edges; cycles are
   rejected server-side.
5. **Quality checks** (`#admin` → Quality) — nine automated rules; errors block
   publish.

Lessons in a fresh deploy ship `generation_status='pending'`; an admin runs batch
generation to produce the served content. Estimated batch cost for the whole
curriculum is ~$1 on Haiku (cached), versus ~27–55 hours of human authoring time.

## Quality-check rules

| Rule             | Level      | What it guards                                                                     |
| ---------------- | ---------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| step-coverage    | error      | declared step types present; at least one `demo`/`audio-clip` and one `reflection` |
| audio-clips      | error      | every `audio-clip` resolves to a real, non-archived clip                           |
| demo-validate    | error      | every `demo` patch validates against the schema manifest                           |
| diagram-sanitize | error      | every SVG/mermaid diagram passes the sanitizer/linter                              |
| word-count       | warn       | per-type word bands (not too thin, not a wall of text)                             |
| prereq-dag       | error      | no cycles, self-edges, or unknown ids; root reachable                              |
| spec-integrity   | error      | `id == track/slug`, real track, valid difficulty, 1–8 objectives                   |
| framing          | warn/error | no prohibited claim phrases; sensitive topics name the evidence                    |
| difficulty       | warn       | a lesson is not easier than its hardest prerequisite                               |

Rules, the DAG check, and the framing lexicon are each defined **once**
(`curriculum_qa.py`, `framing_lexicon.py`) and shared by the spec generator, the
QA pipeline, the graph editor, and CI — the heuristic-drift guard.

## Deferred

Translations/localization · community-contributed lessons · specialized advanced
tracks ("Composing for film", "Live performance technique", v6.4.1+) · video
walkthroughs · downloadable/offline lesson packs · quizzes/assessment · spaced
repetition (v6.4.1). **Never:** certificates of completion (against
calm-by-design).
