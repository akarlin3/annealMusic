Stability of the two-population Sakaguchi–Kuramoto chimera in the (β, A) plane, computed from the reduced order-parameter ODEs (Abrams et al., 2008): the saddle-node (Eq. 17) and Hopf (Eq. 18) series curves (numeric fold and trace-zero locations overplotted as open markers; max deviation 1.4e-03 and 8.7e-04) and the homoclinic curve located by escape-to-sync bisection bound four regions — no chimera, stable stationary chimera, breathing chimera, and post-homoclinic (sync globally attracting) — meeting at the Takens–Bogdanov point (0.2239, 0.3372). The two operating corners both sit at β=0.05: A=0.2 (star) lies in the stable-chimera band (the never-absorbers), while A=0.5 (cross) lies above the homoclinic (A_hc=0.4096) in the post-homoclinic region, where the chimera is only a transient and sync is globally attracting — the regime studied in this work.
